# Monetization Options (Parking Lot)

This document is a dump of monetization directions for Deliberate. It is not a decision.

## Product/UX Led

1) Paid Pro UI layer (dockable panel + threads + diff explorer)
Why it might work: You are selling a tangible UX upgrade that makes the tool feel alive, not just a hook.
Why it might fail: Users can live without UI and will not pay for nice-to-have.

2) Team admin console (policy + audit + backups + overrides)
Why it might work: Companies pay for governance and compliance.
Why it might fail: Needs SSO, reporting, and enterprise expectations.

3) Hosted Deliberate Cloud (inference + analytics)
Why it might work: Recurring revenue with easy upgrades and retention.
Why it might fail: Privacy objections; local-only positioning gets diluted.

4) Freemium CLI + paid UI (UI-only paywall)
Why it might work: Clear upgrade path while keeping OSS goodwill.
Why it might fail: UI becomes the entire product; free remains good enough.

## Distribution/Platform

5) Marketplace cut (paid rule packs, templates, add-ons)
Why it might work: Platform economics if adoption grows.
Why it might fail: No ecosystem without scale.

6) Vendor partnerships (bundled with IDEs/agent CLIs)
Why it might work: Licensing or rev share; built-in distribution.
Why it might fail: Long negotiations and platform politics.

## Risk/Value Pricing

7) Insurance model (pay only when Deliberate saves you)
Why it might work: Emotional ROI and fairness narrative.
Why it might fail: Measuring value is fuzzy and billing gets weird.

8) Per-repo policy keys
Why it might work: Aligns to usage; common in CI and security tools.
Why it might fail: Friction at purchase time for solo developers.

## Unusual / Edge

9) Safety dataset licensing (curated risk patterns + benchmarks)
Why it might work: AI safety and agent vendors buy datasets.
Why it might fail: Data quality and maintenance are hard.

10) Founder’s Edition (lifetime license, early adopters)
Why it might work: Cash infusion and goodwill while pricing is uncertain.
Why it might fail: Caps upside and complicates future pricing.

11) Recovery/incident service
Why it might work: Real money when things go wrong.
Why it might fail: Not scalable, pulls focus from product.

12) Enterprise safety pack (audit exports + compliance reports)
Why it might work: Security buyers pay for auditability and evidence.
Why it might fail: Requires significant enterprise-grade features.

## Open Questions

- If the UI becomes the main paid product, how thin can the OSS core be while still being useful?
- Do we want a strong local-only stance, or is a hosted layer acceptable?
- Is this a devtool business or a security/compliance business?
